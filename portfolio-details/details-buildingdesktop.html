Analysis

I have always been interested in PC part picking and building but I have never had the time or the gut to try it out for myself. When I started my studies at EPHEC I thought that it was the time to try it out, one of my friends wanted to change computer and he also wanted to build it himself we then took time together to chose every part of his computer and got together to build it, even if it was a bit scary at first I loved it! After this multiple people asked me for help and I always agreed so that I could work with as many different PC part makes as possible (Intel, AMD,...), so that I could learn how to use them all.

As I said, it was quite a stressful experience at first, not knowing how fragile everything was or what was possible. It did however become pleasant very fast, learning what is the build order of a computer, seeing a thermal pad for the first time and looking at all the pins of the microprocessors was very interesting. You also learn to be precise, to read the manuals and to make sure that everything is perfect before you pop something in since it isn't cheap. I feel that this experience had made me more rigorous in my work.
